The basics

What is an International Driving Permit?

An International Driving Permit (IDP) is an official document that translates your UK driving licence into other languages, so police and hire-car companies abroad can read it alongside your photocard licence. It doesn't replace your UK licence — you carry both together.

The catch is that different countries recognise different IDP types, set by three international conventions. Some countries accept one type, some another, and a few accept more than one. Carrying the wrong type can mean being turned away at the car-hire desk or fined at the roadside — so getting the right one matters.

  • 1949 Convention

    1949 IDP

    Accepted by many countries including the USA and parts of Asia and Africa.

  • 1968 Convention

    1968 IDP

    The most widely used type across most of Europe and much of the world.

  • 1926 Convention

    1926 IDP

    An older type still required by a small number of countries.

How it really works

An IDP is collected in person — we make sure you're ready

The honest picture

In the UK, an IDP is issued in person, over the counter, at a PayPoint store — and you collect it yourself the same day. It is not available online, and (since 2019) it is no longer issued by the Post Office.

We are not a government issuer and we cannot get the permit for you — it must be issued in person to the licence holder. What we sell is guidance and document checking, with our service fee shown separately from the small official IDP fee you pay at PayPoint.

What we do for you

We take the guesswork out of the trip to PayPoint so it succeeds first time:

  • We confirm which IDP type (1949 / 1968 / 1926) your destination needs.
  • We check your paperwork so nothing's missing or out of date.
  • We tell you exactly what to bring and where to go — your nearest PayPoint store.

What to bring to your PayPoint visit

  • Your full UK photocard driving licence (the holder must attend in person).
  • A recent passport-style photo for the permit.
  • The official IDP fee to pay at the counter (separate from our service fee).
  • If your photocard shows an old address, a valid passport or other ID may be requested — we'll confirm.

Eligibility

Who can get an IDP?

To get an International Driving Permit you must be 18 or over and hold a full UK driving licence (photocard). The permit translates a licence you already hold — so a full, valid UK licence is the starting point.

If your full licence covers the vehicle you'll drive abroad, you're good to go once you have the right IDP type for the country.

Provisional licence holders cannot get an IDP. An IDP can only be issued against a full UK driving licence. If you only hold a provisional licence, you are not eligible — and we'll tell you that honestly rather than take you to a counter that will turn you away.

Good to know

Frequently asked questions

Is this a government service?

No. UKVisaCo is an independent service — not a government website and not a government issuer. We provide guidance and document checking. The IDP itself is an official document issued at PayPoint stores; our service fee is separate from the official IDP fee you pay there.

Can you get the IDP for me?

No — an IDP must be issued in person to you, the licence holder, at a PayPoint store. We can't collect it on your behalf. What we do is confirm which IDP type you need, check and prepare your paperwork, and tell you exactly what to bring and where to go so your visit succeeds first time.

Which countries need which type?

It depends on the country. There are three IDP types, tied to the 1949, 1968 and 1926 conventions. Most of Europe and many other countries use the 1968 type; the USA and several others use the 1949 type; a small number still require the older 1926 type — and some destinations accept more than one. Tell us where you're driving and we'll confirm the exact type (or types) you need before you set off.

What if I only have a provisional licence?

You can't get an IDP on a provisional licence. An IDP can only be issued against a full UK driving licence, because it translates a licence you already hold. If you only have a provisional, you're not eligible yet — we'll tell you straight rather than send you on a wasted trip.
