The basics

Visa vs ETA vs visa-free

Not every trip needs a visa, and "no visa" doesn't always mean "no paperwork." Broadly, a visa is permission you apply and pay for in advance (often as an eVisa linked to your passport); an ETA or travel authorisation (such as the US ESTA or Australian eTA) is a lighter pre-approval you complete online before you fly; and visa-free entry lets eligible passport holders arrive without prior approval, though entry conditions, stay limits and onward-ticket rules can still apply. The right category depends on where you're going and how long you're staying.

By destination

Requirements by destination

Rules are set by the country you're visiting, so the same trip can look very different depending on your destination. Some countries issue eVisas within days; others run authorisation schemes tied to your passport; a few still ask for a sticker or stamp at the border. Driving abroad

International Driving Permits

If you plan to drive on your trip, your UK licence may not be enough on its own. Some countries recognise it directly, while others ask for an International Driving Permit (IDP) to accompany it — and there are different IDP types for different regions, so the one you need depends on where you're going. An IDP is a translation of your licence, not a replacement, and it's only valid alongside your full UK photocard licence.

Before you apply

Documents & passport validity

Most refused or delayed trips come down to documents, not eligibility. Many destinations require your passport to be valid for a set period beyond your return date — commonly several months — and to have one or more blank pages. You may also need a clear digital photo, proof of onward travel, or an address for your stay. Checking these early gives you time to renew a passport or fix a detail before it affects your dates.

The process

Applying & what to expect

Once you know the category and have your documents ready, applying is usually straightforward — but accuracy matters, because small mistakes in names, dates or passport numbers are a common cause of delays. Processing times vary by destination and time of year, so it's wise to apply well ahead of travel rather than the week before. We check every application for errors before it's submitted and keep you updated until it's resolved.
