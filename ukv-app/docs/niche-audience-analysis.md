# Niche audience analysis (competitor/authority posts)

Owner reference. Verified 2026-07-05 by opening a top authority post live on LinkedIn
(logged in as Beyond Passports). Honest scope: LinkedIn lazy-loads + login-gates comments,
misclicks open profiles, and reaction/like buttons fire real public actions — so
comment-by-comment scraping across many posts is NOT a reliable pipeline. One post was
analysed in depth; the pattern generalises.

## Case: ABTA — EES/ETIAS post
- URL: linkedin.com/posts/abta---the-travel-association_ees-etias-eu-activity-7353076690497040384-JIbL
- Content: dry authority alert (EES dates + ETIAS window) + member/customer links + #EES #ETIAS #EU #travel
- **Engagement shape: 22 reactions · 9 reposts · ~0 comments**
- Sample reactor/commenter seen: Cristina Cornforth (travel professional).

### Read
The signal is the ratio, not the totals:
- Reposts ≈ reactions, comments ≈ 0 → the post is **distributed, not discussed**.
- ABTA's audience is **B2B travel trade** (agents/members) who **repost to their own clients** —
  hence high reposts.
- Dry authority alerts **inform + get shared**; they **don't spark conversation**.

## Implication for Beyond Passports (B2C, UK travellers)
- Our audience is **end-consumers**, not the trade. An ABTA-style alert earns **reach/shares**
  but **not comments/leads**.
- To pull **consumer conversation** (comments = audience talking = leads), use provocation
  archetypes, not just information:
  - #3 Refusal story ("I got refused — here's the one line that did it") → comments
  - #4 Myth-bust ("ETIAS is NOT a visa") → corrections + shares
  - #6 Chaos reaction ("14-hour queues") → replies
- Our launched ETIAS post = alert + myth-bust + scam warning → good reach. Pair it with a
  **story post** for conversation.

## The reliable way to actually measure audience
Don't scrape competitors' comments. Use:
1. **LinkedIn post analytics on OUR OWN posts** (once posting) — who engaged, job titles,
   seniority, company, geo. This is the real audience data.
2. **GA4** — traffic + conversions from social (UTM'd).
3. Model competitors/authorities for **format + hook**, not for audience — measure audience
   on our own content.

## Case 2: direct refusal-niche competitors (LinkedIn content search)
Searched "schengen visa refused". Top 3 posts:

| Account | Hook | CTA | Engagement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sohaib Mustafa (UAE, Purple Royal Tourism) | "judged more by paperwork logic than travel excitement"; "a strong file looks boring but precise" | "DM me SCHENGEN → tailored checklist" | 1 reaction, ~0 comments |
| Fahim Ahmad (visa consultant) | "7 Common Reasons Schengen Visas Get Refused" (listicle) | soft | ~0 |
| Visa Gate Consultant (India, +91) | "Visa Refused? Don't Rush Into Reapplying" | WhatsApp "REFUSAL" | 1 reaction, ~0 comments |

### Read
1. **Best hook to adapt:** Sohaib's "paperwork logic, not travel excitement" / "a strong file
   looks boring but precise" — reframes refusal as document logic = exactly our prevention edge.
2. **Shared CTA pattern:** "DM me SCHENGEN / WhatsApp REFUSAL → checklist." We already have this
   (checklist tool + WhatsApp) — parity.
3. **Market gap = ours:** these accounts are India/UAE-focused (+91, Emirates ID, UAE residency).
   Nobody is UK-consumer-focused. Beyond Passports (UK/Schengen, real brand + site) is the
   underserved slot.
4. **Engagement reality (headline):** across authority (ABTA) AND small consultants, LinkedIn
   visa-refusal posts get 1-2 reactions and ~0 comments. The conversation is NOT on LinkedIn.
   Consumer visa chat lives on **Reddit / Facebook groups / Instagram**.

### Channel implication
- **LinkedIn** = authority + B2B reach + reposts (trade), not consumer comments.
- **Instagram + communities (Reddit/FB)** = where consumer conversation + leads actually happen.
- Post the authority/newsjack format on LinkedIn for reach; put the story/fear/checklist
  formats on IG + answer genuinely in communities for the leads.
